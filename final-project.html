Again and Again and Again

I first downloaded Tinder in 2017 with innocent intentions. While it’s hard to recall the details precisely at this point, I remember my friend recommending me to download it because it was a fun little game we could play as we passed our time in our hometown for the summer. Tinder at the time felt like a slightly refined Chat Roulette from my middle school days, and while I was nervous it would invite in creeps, I thought the prospect of using it to have silly conversations with my friends sounded promising enough, and in truth, it was. My friend group and I passed the summer having delightful and atrocious conversations with any boy in a fifty-mile radius. We never went on a real date because that would be way too weird, but mindlessly scrolling and giggling amongst ourselves under the hot white sun was always a pleasant way to pass the time.

Malu Arreguin | UCLA | maluarreguin@g.ucla.edu
